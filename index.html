Why we're building this

The myth of "doing it all alone" is killing us. Whether you're a single parent juggling everything, two parents trying to manage work and family, living with aging parents, or raising kids with your chosen family - the expectation that we should handle it all ourselves is unsustainable. Childcare, elder care, home maintenance, education, emotional support - these were never meant to be solo missions.

We started Village Network because we watched people burn out in isolation. One single parent had a breakdown coordinating three kids' activities across seven WhatsApp groups with no backup. A couple paid strangers from TaskRabbit $80/hour to fix things their neighbor could have done in 20 minutes. A grandmother caring for her grandchildren had no way to connect with other grandparents nearby doing the same thing.

"It takes a village" - not a nuclear family, not a gig economy app, not doing it alone. We're building the infrastructure to bring villages back.

Technology should bring us together, not replace human connection with transactions. That's why Village Network will always be free, will never show ads, will never sell your data, and will never insert strangers into your trusted circle.

We believe in abundance over scarcity - your neighbor has the tools you need, you have the skills they need, and together you have more capacity than any of you do alone. We believe in reciprocity over currency - relationships strengthen when we keep track of mutual aid, not when we pretend every exchange must be perfectly balanced. We believe in trust through vouching - your friend's judgment is worth more than a stranger's 5-star rating.

This isn't just an app. It's a bet that given the right tools, people will choose community over isolation, cooperation over competition, and relationships over transactions. No matter what your family looks like.
