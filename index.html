How you build your tribe

Starting with trust, growing through vouching, organizing collective knowledge

1

Start with your inner circle

Invite 3-5 people you already trust - neighbors, friends, family. They're your founding members. Each person you invite can see and connect with each other.

Example: You invite Sarah (neighbor), Mike (brother), and Lisa (friend from work). They can now see each other's profiles and what they're offering.
2

Each member vouches for others

When Sarah wants to add her cousin Tom, she vouches for him. You see: "Sarah vouches for Tom" with context about how they know each other. You can accept, decline, or meet Tom first.

Example: "Sarah (your neighbor, trusted for 8 months) vouches for Tom (her cousin, teacher, has 2 kids). Tom offers: guitar lessons, camping gear, weekend hiking trips."
3

The network becomes a web

Over time, you have: direct connections (people you invited), extended connections (vouched by your direct connections), and your tribe's collective knowledge - who has what, who knows what, who's good at what.

Your tribe's collective resources:

How the network grows

1

Start with who you trust

Invite 3-5 friends, neighbors, or family. Each person brings their trusted circle. The network expands through vouching, not algorithms.

2

Give before you receive

Offer what you can: childcare, tools, skills, rides. Every contribution builds your time bank and strengthens the web. New members start by giving.

3

The network matches you

Need something? The system finds the best fit from your trusted circle based on availability, proximity, and reciprocity balance. Accept, decline, or counter-offer.
